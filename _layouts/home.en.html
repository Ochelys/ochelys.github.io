TEA, The Ebook Alternative

User research for an e-commerce platform (Android, iOS, e-readers)

TEA, The Ebook Alternative

Context

TEA wanted to decrease customer churn rate.

The team wanted to build new and improved mobile applications (Android / iOS) and revamp their e-commerce website, to give customers the best buying and reading experience they could find.

We organised user testing sessions in bookshops to learn how the sales funnel performed, how easy it was for customers to create their account and synchronise ebooks onto their e-reader, as well as e-reading applications.

We ran a total of 27 scenario-based tests in 2 different bookshops, studying 3 different devices (Android tablet, iPad, and e-reader).

Results

User feedback revealed areas where the UI underperformed. We recommended several changes, and pointed out a major hurdle that prevented wide adoption.

Thanks to our intervention, TEA redefined part of their annual roadmap and redesigned their applications to be much more user-friendly.

Groupama

Interactive prototype for an internal documentation search engine

Groupama

Context

Groupama SA is the parent company behind Groupama insurance, one of France's leading insurance companies.

The Quality, Methods and Budget department wanted to give everyone in the group an easy way to find internal documents and processes.

Before actually building the tool, they wanted to validate user interest, make sure that it would be highly usable and adapted to actual user needs.

Actions

We set up a user-centered approach to better understand user needs, and created an interactive HTML/CSS prototype.

  • Creation of personas and usage scenarios with the project lead
  • Shadowing and user interviews in Lyon and Bordeaux
  • Wireframe design
  • Mockup design
  • Interactive prototype in HTML5 and CSS3

Results

The interactive prototype was used as blueprint by the development team to build a functional prototype.

Prototypes allows testing user adaptation and adoption and is a particularly useful first step to a large project. It also simplifies internal discussion and idea sharing.

Oolys

Oolys is a serious game for children in orthodental care.

Oolys

Context

Patient motivation is crucial in orthodental care, because children need to clean the braces and wear the rubber bands daily for the treatment to be fast and efficient.

But children typically use braces between ages 10 to 13, an age at which they feel quite strongly against such constraints. As a result, treatments often end up taking more time than necessary and costing more to their parents and the Health Insurance system.

Situation

Most motivation programs today incentivize children by giving them toys and other similar rewards, some even offering prizes as expensive as game consoles.

But giving some children such large rewards can be controversial when the Health Insurance system is striving to reduce treatment costs, and also creates inequalities between children whose parents spend an otherwise equivalent amount of money.

Our solution

Oolys is our attempt at solving both problems at once, by giving children an adventure book whose story depends on their implication in their treatment.

At each appointment the orthodontic team assesses the child's braces' status, which corresponds to a number of points. The story then evolves according to the number of points gained and the child gets reward stickers to use or collect.

Antepulsio II

Self-learning application for anatomy and biomechanics, designed for desktop computers, tablets and smartphones.

Antepulsio II

Project description

The aim of this project is to give medical and sports students an interactive tool to better understand how muscles, joints and connective tissues work together, using realistic interactive 3D renderings.

Financing

The total cost of the project is an estimated €1.8M, with development planned to cover the span of 3 years. It has been approved by EuroBioMed and Imaginov, and will receive €1M states and local grants. We will partner with Lyon 1 university, Itycom, brûle., and Anatoscope.
